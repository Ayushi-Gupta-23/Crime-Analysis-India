Murder in India remains a pressing concern, with factors such as social, economic, and cultural dynamics influencing its prevalence. Comprehensive analysis is vital to understand the underlying causes and develop effective strategies for prevention and law enforcement.

Suicides

Suicide rates in India vary significantly across states and have been a subject of extensive analysis. Factors such as economic disparities, social pressures, and access to mental healthcare play a pivotal role in these variations. In-depth state-level analysis is essential for targeted interventions and mental health support initiatives.

Female Foeticide and Selling of Girls for Prostitution

Female foeticide and the trafficking of girls for prostitution are grave issues in India. These practices reflect deeply rooted gender biases and pose significant threats to the rights and dignity of women and girls. Eradicating these harmful practices requires comprehensive social and legal measures to address gender inequality and protect the vulnerable.
